PROJECT 01 / CAR CLEANING PRODUCT

Car Vacuum Cleaner: turning market positioning into two clear product concepts.

User & market researchCompetitive analysisModeling and rendering
This case starts with positioning: user needs and competitor signals were translated into concepts that could be evaluated, priced, and communicated.

Problem framing

The in-car cleaning scenario was broken into four demand areas: performance, compact-space use, EV compatibility, and sustainable materials. That logic shaped two product lines: TurboForce X1 for performance and EcoPulse S1 for responsibility.

PROJECT 02 / MANUAL COFFEE GRINDER

57 survey responses focused the grinder redesign on portability, effort, and grind quality.

Survey launchUser needs synthesisData visualization
This case moves closer to user research: surveys, observation, and interviews turned scattered feedback into a clear product direction.

Research takeaways

The respondent group was mainly 18-25-year-old students and lifestyle-oriented coffee users. They valued grind control, aroma retention, ritual, and portability, while also reporting cleaning difficulty, physical effort, unclear adjustment, and inconsistent grinding.

Portability / carry the setup outdoors Efficiency / reduce hand-cranking fatigue Grind Quality / improve consistency Ritual / preserve the brewing experience
Project 0205

INSIGHT TO PRODUCT

Visualized needs became the decision basis for an all-in-one grinder cup.

Output and impact

Survey and interview findings were synthesized into user profiles, pain-point priorities, and feature directions. The final concept connected bean storage, controlled release, a foldable handle, cup integration, and recyclable materials into one coherent product position.

This project reflects my ability to translate unstructured user feedback into an actionable design brief, with clear priorities and presentable evidence.

PROJECT 04 / SUSTAINABLE MATERIALS

Sustainable Materials Research: turning fruit waste and plant fibers into testable material hypotheses.

Experiment executionProcess documentationObservation synthesis
The focus then shifts to sustainable materials: this case highlights experimental documentation, failure analysis, and evidence-based iteration.

ESG perspective

The project was approached through a waste-to-material lens, testing how mangosteen rind, gelatin, glycerin, vinegar, lemon juice, and plant fibers could become tactile, moldable, and narratable bio-composites.

  • Clarify material origin and circular value
  • Use experiment records to support the sustainability story
  • Treat failed samples as evidence for optimization
Project 0410

EXPERIMENT LOGIC

The value of experiment logs is explaining why something failed and how the next round should improve.

How documentation drove iteration

Ratios, process deviations, texture, smell, elasticity, cracking, and mold were tracked across trials. Those records made the next changes evidence-based instead of intuitive.
